# GeoJournal — Slide Talk Track

The same lines are embedded in the PowerPoint as presenter notes.

## Fast five-minute route

Present slides **1–7**, show the live product, then jump to **19** for the closing question. Slides **8–18** are product-proof backup; slides **20–27** are the roadmap appendix.

## Slide-by-slide lines

1. **Opening** — Travel has become more important after COVID, when the laptop became the office and people began valuing freedom and experiences differently. GeoJournal gives every journey its own living world instead of leaving it scattered in storage.
2. **Problem depth** — Frequent travelers capture photos, locations and stories, but the context is split across camera rolls, Maps, journals and chats. The memory exists; what is missing is one useful system around it.
3. **Delta-4** — Four disconnected places become one reusable Atlas. Import once, then retrieve, rediscover, plan and share from the same consented memory graph.
4. **Built today** — The full Atlas loop is interactive today. OpenAI is configuration-dependent, Dodo is Test Mode, and the Maximem private-memory bridge is the next infrastructure step.
5. **Global day zero** — GeoJournal creates value for one traveler before any network exists. Anyone can bring history, review suggestions, relive it and plan from it because the model is coordinate-first.
6. **US GTM** — Start with outdoor clubs, digital nomads, creators and study-abroad groups. The activation is a populated Atlas, and every shared memory or co-traveler invite brings the next user.
7. **Business model** — Start simply at $6 monthly or $49 annually, so normal posting and memory storage remain easy. Creator commerce follows only after trust.
8. **Core Atlas** — The high-resolution 3D globe has a familiar 2D fallback, 17 personal memories and 10 clearly authored friend pins. Mine and Circle switch between a private and shared world.
9. **Onboarding** — The journey covers landing, account creation, username, avatar and theme. Imports stay review-first, and the tour makes the 3D product easy to understand.
10. **Cold start** — Google Timeline or geotagged Instagram exports become suggestions. The user accepts or rejects every pin before anything enters the Atlas.
11. **Search and Rediscover** — Meaning-aware search finds memories by intent, while On This Day and Past Julys intentionally bring old journeys back.
12. **Nexus AI** — Nexus reads accepted Atlas memories and Trip Fit details such as home city, month, leave and budget. The deployed demo uses a labelled sandbox fallback unless OpenAI is configured.
13. **Social and badges** — Travelers can follow explorers, open friend globes and accept a shared-trip invitation before it appears on their Atlas. Badges and themes turn progress into travel identity.
14. **Pro and creator economy** — Pro is a predictable subscription rather than a credit meter. Atlas Market later supports creator guides, route packs and group trips.
15. **Inspiration** — Mood filters, authored stories and a Why It Fits signal help users borrow a feeling rather than copy an itinerary. The presentation community is seeded.
16. **Expeditions** — Creators can preview small-group journeys with route, dates, seats and budget together. This is a concept surface, not bookable inventory.
17. **Atlas Market** — Creators can package itineraries, routes and local experiences. One listing opens a real Dodo Test Mode checkout; fulfilment and payouts are next.
18. **Technology stack** — React, TypeScript and Vite power the interface; Three.js/react-globe.gl and Leaflet power the maps; Firebase handles identity, data and hosting.
19. **Close** — The first customers already curate their journeys. Ask: what would make you trust GeoJournal with your complete travel history?
20. **Trust foundation** — Move private memory retrieval behind a consented Maximem server bridge, add export/deletion controls and connect production Dodo entitlements.
21. **Trip Rooms** — Two or more travelers and Nexus plan together, settle dates and budgets, then accept the final shared memory.
22. **Smart Availability** — Calendar, holidays, leave balance, month and budget help Nexus recommend a trip the user can actually take.
23. **Mobile and alerts** — PWA first, then native apps. A follower notification deep-links directly to the new public memory.
24. **Travel Quests** — Long-term goals become collectible, from the Seven Wonders to Jyotirlingas, Pushkarams and annual targets.
25. **Creator economy** — Guides expand into verified packages and trip groups, with Dodo payouts and clearly labelled promotions.
26. **Intelligence moat** — The moat is the permissioned graph of accepted, rejected and revisited memories—not a generic model.
27. **Atlas Persona** — Travel history becomes visual identity through generated portraits, themes, passports and evolving profile collections.
